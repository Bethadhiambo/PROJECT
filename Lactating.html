#Lactating#

Proper nutrition for lactating women is essential in combating malnutrition because it directly influences both the mother’s health and the quality of breast milk, which is a critical source of nutrition for newborns. A nutrient-rich diet ensures adequate production of breast milk, providing essential nutrients like proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals that promote the baby’s growth and immune system development. Malnutrition in lactating women can lead to reduced milk supply, nutrient deficiencies in both mother and child, and increased risk of illness. Ensuring a balanced intake of calories, protein, calcium, and vitamins like A, D, and B12 is key to maintaining the health of both mother and baby, supporting post-pregnancy recovery, and reducing the risk of long-term nutritional deficiencies.
